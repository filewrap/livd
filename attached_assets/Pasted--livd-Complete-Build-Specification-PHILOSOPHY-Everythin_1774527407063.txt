# `.livd` — Complete Build Specification

---

## PHILOSOPHY
Everything starts from a dot. Everything ends at a dot. Destruction is not failure — it is the condition for revival. The website does not guide the user. It lives in front of them. The user witnesses. Nothing is explained unless asked.

## CURSOR — THE DOT COMPANION

A white dot, 6px diameter, follows mouse at all times. Not a pointer replacement — rendered as a DOM element, `position: fixed`, `border-radius: 50%`, `pointer-events: none`, `z-index: 9999`.

**Behavior states:**
- **Default:** smooth follow with 80ms lerp lag. Slow, weighted.
- **Idle 3s+:** slowly pulses — scales 6px → 10px → 6px, 2s loop. Breathing.
- **Near breaking geometry (section 02):** subtle random offset tremor, ±3px every 100ms
- **During destruction:** leaves a fading trail. Every 50ms a ghost dot spawns at current position, opacity 0.3, fades to 0 over 600ms. Trail only exists during destruction.
- **During formation/revival:** gets pulled 15–20px toward main object center. Lerp toward object, not mouse. Feels like gravity. Returns to mouse when scroll unlocks.
- **During enemy phase:** dot color flickers to enemy color (deep red `#8B0000`) for 200ms, returns white. Happens once when first enemy appears.
- **Dynamic Island speaks:** dot pulses once — quick scale 6px → 14px → 6px over 300ms.
- **`.end` white fill:** dot is last element to go white. All else fills first. Dot holds black for 1 extra second, then fades white over 500ms.

## DYNAMIC ISLAND

**Structure:** A pill-shaped div. `position: fixed`, `top: 20px`, `left: 50%`, `transform: translateX(-50%)`. Black fill `#0a0a0a`, subtle border `1px solid #1a1a1a`. `border-radius: 999px`. `overflow: hidden`.

**Default state:** `width: 120px`, `height: 36px`. Nearly invisible on black. Contains nothing visible — just exists.

**Expansion:** GSAP `to()` animation. Width expands from center, height slightly increases. Text fades in after expansion completes. Never snaps — always eases. `ease: "power2.out"`, duration `0.4s`.

**Text style:** Space Mono, `font-size: 11px`, `letter-spacing: 0.05em`, color `#ffffff` at 70% opacity. Never bold. Always lowercase.

**Collapse:** after message duration (varies), text fades out first, then pill collapses back. `ease: "power2.in"`, duration `0.3s`.

**Messages by state:**

```
Scroll locked (formation)  → expands to 200px  → "wait."
Scroll attempt while locked → quick pulse, expands → "formation in progress"
Destruction trigger         → expands to 220px  → "an error occurred."  [pause 800ms]  "going home →"
Fast scroll in hero         → expands to 180px  → "slow down."
Idle 30s+                   → expands to 160px  → "still here?"
Enemy objects appear        → expands to 140px, border flickers red briefly → "resistance"
Main object wins            → quick green border pulse → "rebuilt"
Scroll unlocks              → expands to 180px  → "you can move now"
Near .end                   → island shrinks to 8px circle → stays as dot → disappears
```

**Island never overlaps content forcefully.** It is awareness, not interruption.

## LOADING SCREEN

Full viewport. `#000000`. Centered.

**Animation:** A dot rendered in Three.js or pure CSS. Starts as nothing. Particles converge inward forming a complete circle/sphere outline. Holds for 300ms. Then breaks apart outward. Loops.

Loop timing: form over `1.2s` → hold `300ms` → break over `0.8s` → reset `200ms` → repeat.

**Behind loading screen:** all Three.js scenes, geometries, fonts, textures load silently.

**Completion:** when all assets loaded, current loop completes its break animation, does not interrupt mid-form. Then loading dot does one final form → instead of breaking, it **fills solid** → expands to fill viewport black → fades out revealing the hero. Seamless.

## SECTION 00 — HERO

**Scene setup:** Three.js scene, black background. Camera slightly angled, not flat. Ambient light low. One directional light from upper-left, white, intensity 0.8.

**"3D" text:**
- Rendered with `TextGeometry`, `helvetiker_regular`, size `1.5`, depth `0.4` (actual extrusion depth — this is genuinely 3D)
- Material: `MeshStandardMaterial`, color `#ffffff`, metalness `0.3`, roughness `0.6`
- Enters: fades in over `600ms` while slowly rotating toward camera from 15° angle
- Holds for `1.5s`
- **Wipe/Thanos effect:** particles. On trigger, geometry converts to particle cloud (~2000 points sampled from surface). Particles drift rightward and upward with slight randomness, opacity fades individually over `1.2s`. Not simultaneous — left side starts first, right side last. Like being erased by a moving force.

**"website" text:**
- Same TextGeometry. Size `1.2`, depth `0.3`
- Material: `MeshStandardMaterial`, color `#cccccc`
- Enters from below — y position animates up, opacity fades in over `500ms`
- **Melt effect:** vertices of geometry begin drifting downward individually over `2s`. Not uniform — some vertex groups fall faster, some slower. Geometry deforms visually. Color shifts slightly warmer as it melts, toward `#888888`. Finally drops below viewport.

**"welcome" text:**
- Size `1.8`, depth `0.5`
- Material: `MeshStandardMaterial`, color `#8B0000` (brutal dark red). Emissive `#3d0000` low glow.
- Enters: slams in from slight z-distance forward. Fast. `0.3s`. Feels aggressive.
- **Char break:** each character is a separate mesh. After `1s` hold, chars explode outward with random velocity vectors. Each char rotates rapidly on all axes while flying. No two chars go same direction. Velocity: fast initial, then slow drag over `1.5s`. They do not disappear — they scatter to random irrelevant positions and hold there, slowly rotating in place.
- After chars scatter — the remaining space where "welcome" was is empty. Chars still faintly visible at edges rotating.
- All scattered chars then individually fade out over `2s`, random start times.

**Screen blank transition:**
- All geometry gone. Pure black.
- 1.5s hold. Absolute silence. Nothing moves. Cursor still breathing.
- This pause is intentional. Do not fill it.

## SECTION 01 — PASSIVE SCROLL ZONE

User scrolls down naturally. Scene is a Three.js environment — dark, ambient, feels like empty space.

**Background geometry:** 3–4 abstract low-poly shapes floating at distance. Barely visible at first. `MeshStandardMaterial` dark grey `#111111`. Slowly rotating on their own axes.

**As user scrolls:**
- Scroll progress `0–40%`: shapes get slightly brighter, camera drifts forward slowly
- Scroll progress `40–70%`: shapes begin developing cracks. Achieved via morph targets — a cracked version of each geometry morphs in gradually. Crack lines appear as emissive `#ffffff` at very low intensity.
- Scroll progress `70–90%`: cracks widen. One shape loses a chunk — a fragment breaks off and slowly drifts. Cursor trembles.
- Scroll progress `90–100%`: Island expands — `"an error occurred."` — 800ms pause — `"going home →"`
- **Auto scroll:** `window.scrollTo({ top: 0, behavior: 'smooth' })`. Camera pulls back. Everything on screen cracks fully. Destruction triggers.

**No text in this section. No labels. No explanation.** The island is the only communication.

---

## SECTION 02 — DESTRUCTION

Triggered by auto-scroll return. Not a section user manually reaches — it overtakes the hero zone.

**All hero remnants die:**
- Scattered "welcome" chars that didn't fully fade — they shatter into smaller fragments
- Background shapes fully collapse into particle clouds
- Particles from all sources converge briefly at center then explode outward past viewport edges
- Camera shakes once — single frame offset `±5px` — then holds
- One last particle from the explosion trails back to center slowly. Lands. Stops.
- That is the first dot.

---

## SECTION 03 — REVIVAL

**Scroll locked immediately.** Island: `"wait."`

**Dot system:**
- First dot: white, 4px rendered sphere in Three.js, appears at center. Pulses gently.
- `1.2s` later: second dot appears at random position within 100px radius of center
- Dots continue appearing — timing gets slightly faster as count grows. By dot 15, they're appearing every `400ms`
- Each dot: unique size variation `3px–7px`, slight brightness variation

**First dot behavior (main dot):**
- When dot count reaches 8, main dot begins slowly moving toward nearest dot
- On contact: absorbs it. Size increases slightly. Line briefly flashes between them before absorption.
- Main dot now moves toward next nearest. Pattern continues.
- Movement is not straight — slight curved path, organic.

**Resisting dots:**
- 20–30% of dots have resistance flag. When main dot approaches within 30px, they move away slowly.
- If main dot catches them anyway — absorption. If they escape past 150px from center — they stop, flicker, then their opacity drops to 0 over `1s`. They die. No absorption. No line flash.
- Dead dot leaves a faint ring that expands and fades — like a ripple.

**Object formation:**
- As main dot absorbs, new geometry begins forming around it. Not a preset shape.
- Built procedurally: each absorption adds a vertex or edge to a growing `BufferGeometry`. Shape is determined by absorption order + random variance seed generated at session start (this seed produces the unique `#a3f.91` style code, stored in memory only).
- Result: no two users see the same object. Asymmetric, jagged, organic. Not beautiful by design — it just is what it is.
- Object material: `MeshStandardMaterial`, color `#e8e8e8`, wireframe partially. Low emissive.

**Seed generation:**
- 6 chars. Alphanumeric. Generated from: timestamp milliseconds + absorption order array + dead dot count. Hashed to 6 chars.
- Displayed nowhere obvious. Embedded faintly in the object's geometry as a barely-visible texture or just exists in memory. User never told about it explicitly. It simply is.

---

## SECTION 04 — CONFLICT

Formation reaches ~70% completion. New objects spawn.

**Spawn behavior:**
- 5–8 new objects. Each a simple geometry (icosahedron, torus, box — randomized). Appear at viewport edges and drift inward.
- Initial color: same grey as main object. Neutral.
- After `2s` drift: 40–50% of them shift color to deep red `#6B0000` → `#8B0000`. This transition takes `800ms`. Smooth. No warning.
- Enemy objects: continue drifting toward main object aggressively.

**Conflict mechanics:**
- When enemy touches main object: main object geometry distorts slightly at contact point. A chunk visually compresses. Emissive flickers.
- Main object "fights" — a burst of particles emits from contact point, enemy object fragments. Its pieces scatter and fade.
- Each fight: main object loses a small piece. A fragment breaks off and drifts away slowly.
- Cursor flickers `#8B0000` on first contact.
- Island: `"resistance"` with brief red border.

**Winning:**
- All enemies defeated. Main object is visibly damaged — asymmetric, pieces missing, some edges jagged.
- Island brief green border flash: `"rebuilt"`
- But it is not rebuilt yet. That word is premature. It is a promise.

---

## SECTION 05 — REBUILDING

Main object begins self-repair. Lost fragments reverse their drift — float back toward object. Reattach with a brief light flash at join point.

Repair is not perfect. Object regains volume but remains asymmetric. The scars are geometry — the shape is permanently altered by what happened. No two users see the same damaged-and-repaired form.

New objects begin spawning around the main object — neutral, small, varied geometries. They orbit loosely. No threat. They just exist alongside.

**Scroll unlocks.** Island shrinks back to default pill. `"you can move now"` — fades quickly.

---

## SECTION 06 — SURVIVAL STATE

Main object holds center. Orbiting objects drift. Scene breathes — very slow camera drift in a wide arc.

Cursor breathing is most visible here. Slowest section. Intentionally calm after chaos.

Seed code exists somewhere in this scene. Not labeled. Could be rendered as faint geometry texture, or as particle arrangement that spells it if user looks long enough. Or not visible at all — it just exists. Developer's choice on visibility.

User can freely scroll here. Nothing breaks. Nothing threatens.

---

## SECTION 07 — .end

**Trigger:** user scrolls to bottom of survival state, or a timer after sufficient dwell time — whichever comes first.

**Dot from path:**
- A single white dot appears at a random edge position (top/left/right/bottom — random each time)
- Moves toward center via a non-straight path. Slight curves, like it's navigating. Speed: deliberate. Not slow, not fast. `3–4s` travel time.
- All existing objects notice it — they don't move, but their emissive flickers once as it passes near them. Like acknowledgment.

**"end" text:**
- As dot reaches center, stops.
- `TextGeometry`, `helvetiker_regular`, lowercase `"end"`. Size `0.8`. Depth `0.2`.
- Births from the dot itself — starts at dot scale (`0`), grows outward over `1s`. Eases out.
- Color: white. No emissive. Clean.
- Holds for `2–3s`.

**White fill:**
- Dot (the traveling dot, now at center) begins expanding. It is a sphere mesh scaling up.
- Scale grows non-linearly — slow start, accelerates.
- Color: pure white `#ffffff`.
- As sphere expands past objects — objects it touches turn white, lose their geometry definition, become white silhouettes, then absorbed into the white.
- `"end"` text holds until white sphere reaches it — then it too turns white and merges.
- White fills entire viewport. `background-color` transitions to `#ffffff` at the moment Three.js sphere fills frame — seamless handoff.
- Cursor dot holds black for `1000ms` after full white. Last dark thing. Then fades to white over `500ms`.
- `1500ms` of pure white silence.
- Island has already shrunk to dot and disappeared before `.end` triggered.
- Nothing. Page is white. Cursor is gone.

## HINT SYSTEM

**Trigger:** A small pill button, bottom-right. `position: fixed`. Label: a single dot `·` by default. On hover expands to show `hint`. Space Mono, tiny. Nearly invisible — `opacity: 0.3`, on hover `opacity: 0.8`.

**What it reveals:**
A small panel slides up from bottom-right. Dark `#0d0d0d` background, `1px solid #1f1f1f` border, `border-radius: 12px`, `padding: 16px`, max-width `240px`.

Content:
```
.livd

before destruction,
something always exists.
scroll slowly.
some things resist.
some things die.
the object you see
has never existed before.
it will not exist again.

·
```

Text in Space Mono, `11px`, `line-height: 1.8`, color `#666666`. The dot at end is `#ffffff`.

**Panel appears:** slides up `translateY(20px → 0)` with fade, `0.4s ease`.
**Closes:** click dot button again or click outside.
